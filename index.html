The Real Problem

You're not stuck because you're incapable. You're stuck because you're overthinking.

Smart, capable people get derailed by tiny setbacks and spend days, weeks, months stuck in moments that should have lasted ninety seconds. Then they wonder why momentum keeps slipping.

The pause after the setback lasts longer than the setback itself. The pause after the win isn't any kinder. Recovery speed is the real skill no one taught you.

  • Athletes who can't shake the last missed point.
  • Entrepreneurs who overthink decisions until the opportunity walks.
  • Parents who spiral over every misstep instead of moving on.
  • High performers who land the win and instantly ask, "was that a fluke?"
The So What Promise

What if you could recover in ninety seconds instead of ninety days?

That's not a metaphor. Neuroscience says emotions physiologically dissipate in roughly ninety seconds — if you stop feeding them with thoughts. The rest is the story you keep telling yourself.

The Method

The So What Sequence

Five moves that compress recovery from days to under a minute. Repeatable in any setback, any win, any "what now?" moment.

1

Identify

Strip away the story. Name the actual fact, not the narrative spiraling around it. Thirty seconds, max.

2

Decide

One clear action. The most direct path from where you are to the next right move. Not the perfect one — the next one.

3

Act

Now. Not tomorrow. Not when you feel ready. You can course-correct while moving, but you can't steer a parked car.

4

Adjust

Treat resistance as data, not a verdict. There's no such thing as failure — only feedback.

5

Move On

Acknowledge the result. Extract the lesson. Then say it: so what's next?

"You can't think your way into momentum. You move your way into it."
